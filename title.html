CHANGELOG

// VERSION HISTORY

v3.16.0 — GRAPHICS REWORK
A full visual pass over the whole theatre. The world got deeper: two hazy parallax mountain ranges now roll behind the front line, and the terrain itself grew biome scenery — conifer forests in the wetlands, leafy brush on the plains, dry scrub & pebbles in the deserts, boulders on the rocky heights and glinting snowfields on the peaks (it all keeps clear of your cities). Water is alive: rolling swells, a bright surface crest, drifting sun glints and foam washing at every shoreline. The sky got a ceiling worth scrolling to: a cratered gibbous MOON, slow emerald AURORA curtains rippling through the upper atmosphere, occasional shooting stars through the exosphere, and an anamorphic lens streak on the sun. Combat hits harder on the eyes: explosions now throw glowing EMBERS that arc and gutter out, shockwave rings bloom additively, smoke puffs are lit with a bright crown over a dark body, and young fireballs burn white-hot at the core. The title-screen Earth was remastered too — night-side city lights glitter as continents roll into darkness, the ocean catches a specular sun glint, a sunlit rim wraps the day side, and tiny satellites blink along tilted orbits. A soft vignette frames it all. (Aurora, meteors & planet spin respect the REDUCED MOTION option.) Zoomed-in scrolling fixed: the camera clamps are now zoom-aware, so while zoomed in you can finally pan all the way to the ground, the world's edges and the bottom of the subterranean layer; pan speed matches what the eye sees at any zoom, and the minimap's white viewport box tracks the true visible span.

v3.15.0 — STABILITY ECONOMY REWORK
A big rebalance of the political economy to smooth the difficulty curve. Influence now comes ONLY from Stability — the higher your stability, the faster research & army cap grow. Stability no longer ticks up freely; it climbs slowly toward a STABILITY CAP (shown as cur / cap up top). Comms Towers, Propaganda Bureaus & Radar now raise that CAP instead of granting flat stability/influence. Dangerous-research ⚠ unrest now recovers on its own over time, so you're never permanently walled out. Fixes: your own Mind-Forge plasma column no longer fries your own troops; Arsenal Ships / Ballistic Subs now build at the water surface instead of briefly appearing on the seabed. Fire Control got a full Field Manual entry (open the manual) — it works; it just needed explaining.

v3.14.0 — GLOBAL PROJECTILES & CLEARER GUNS
Projectiles now live everywhere on the map, not just near your camera — battles on a far front fully resolve even while you're watching somewhere else (shells, missiles, rail slugs and rockets used to be culled off-screen). Also clarified the Fire Control guns: select a heavy gun (Bunker, Laser Tower, Orbital Gun, Mass Driver, Microrocket Battery, Particle Cannon), hit ⊕ ADD TO FIRE CONTROL, then click incoming enemies inside its radar to designate them — the gun then fires only at what you mark (right-click / Esc releases). Stale "manual control" wording on a few guns was corrected to match.

v3.13.1 — BUG FIXES
Fixes from an independent code review: explosions near a city no longer damage it from high altitude (blast splash now respects vertical distance to city districts, like it already did for units); the research detail panel shows each building's real artwork instead of a generic factory box; and the research tech-tree time estimate now accounts for game speed.

v3.13.0 — AFTER-ACTION REPORT
Polish pass: winning or losing now ends on a proper battle summary instead of a bare title. The victory/defeat screen tallies drones destroyed, waves repelled, cities held, districts & forces lost, and time in combat, topped with a performance commendation (hold every city untouched for a ★★★ Flawless Defense). Gives each battle a real payoff and a score to beat.

v3.12.0 — DEFLECTOR TECH PATH
Deflector Shields is now a combination of the Point Defense and Directed Energy upgrades — it sits downstream of both on the research tree and requires you to have researched each before you can unlock the Shield Generator. A deflector field is the fusion of an interceptor's targeting and a beam weapon's projector tech, so the path now reflects that.

v3.11.0 — CAMERA & UI
Two quality-of-life controls. Zoom: roll the mouse wheel (or use the +/−/⌂ buttons, bottom-right) to zoom in toward the cursor and back out — get right down on your units to watch them fight, or pull back to the full view. (Shift+wheel still pans the tall sky vertically.) Minimize the build bar: the ⚙ BUILD header now has a ▾ arrow that collapses the whole build panel down to a small tab so it's out of your way — click ▸ to bring it back.

v3.10.0 — DEFENSE & DESTRUCTION
The rod apocalypse has answers now. A new DEFENSE research line: Point Defense (Aegis Battery — rapid flak that swats incoming missiles & tungsten rods), Microrockets (rockets that split into several INDEPENDENTLY-homing submunitions — re-arms your strike aircraft & unlocks the Microrocket Battery), and Deflector Shields (Shield Generators that dome your forces — rods, missiles & bombs splash off them until battered down, then recharge). Plus Particle Beams off the energy line — Particle Cannons fire single hypervelocity bolts that punch through anything at any altitude. And dispersed ballistic platforms — a road-mobile Launcher (TEL), an offshore Arsenal Ship and a Ballistic Submarine — so your strike arsenal isn't all in one silo waiting to be glassed; plant them in important spots.
Enemies now break apart by what kills them: lasers slag & drop them intact; guns leave them smoking & riddled before they fall; missiles pop and tumble out of the sky; plasma blows the body off the shot with spall trailing behind; railguns & particle bolts split them clean in half with a debris cloud. Explosions got a bright bloom and leave black smoke rising to fill the sky.
Fixes: tungsten-rod bombardment now rains on cities & your infrastructure across the WHOLE map — no longer just wherever your camera is looking; and the Cluster MIRV now reliably splits at the top of its arc instead of occasionally fizzling. The Mass-Driver Railway moved down to Orbital Operations (earlier access).

v3.9.0 — FIRE CONTROL
Manual gun control is now a real fire-direction system. Select a heavy gun and hit ⊕ ADD TO FIRE CONTROL — then select others and add them too, building a whole battery under your command. The group projects a radar over its combined range (range rings + a sweeping scope), and you click each incoming enemy to designate it as a target — designated foes get a spinning target reticle, and every gun in the group automatically engages the marked targets that fall within its reach (so high-altitude designations are taken by your railguns & orbital guns, ground ones by your bunkers). Click a target again to drop it; right-click or Esc releases the whole battery. Designate the tungsten rod streaking toward your capital and watch the whole line swing onto it. Also: the Mass-Driver Railway moved down to Orbital Operations — it's now an early long-range emplacement, no Orbital Command research required.

v3.8.0 — PRODUCTION CHAINS
Your biggest units are now ASSEMBLED from a real industrial chain, not conjured from raw IC. Two new component parts — Heavy Alloy (poured by Factories, faster by the Heavy Foundry) and Rocket Parts (built by Space Centers & Space Stations) — accumulate in stock over time, and you SPEND them to build the big stuff: Heavy & Super-Heavy tanks cost alloy; every orbital unit costs rocket parts. Orbital units also need ORBITAL LIFT capacity — each Space Center provides 5 lift (a Space Station +8) and every satellite/frigate/dreadnought reserves some while it's in orbit, so a larger space fleet means building more launch infrastructure (the "needs N orbital capacity to lift" rule). And Special Resources are now true enablers, not just discounts: the Railgun needs a STEEL deposit to build at all — claim the ground that has what your war machine runs on. The PRODUCE panel shows your live lift & parts, each unit card lists what it consumes (greying out when you're short), and the 📊 STATS panel tracks your stockpiles.

v3.7.0 — TAKE THE TRIGGER
You can now take manual control of your heavy guns. Select a Bunker, Laser Tower, Nanite Turret, Orbital Gun or Mass-Driver Railway and hit ⊕ MANUAL CONTROL — the gun stops firing on its own and hands you the trigger: a dashed aim line follows your cursor to a targeting reticle, and you click to fire wherever you point (it still has to reload between shots). Snipe a descending dropship out of the exosphere, walk railgun rounds across a landing, or hold fire and pick your moment. Right-click or Esc (or ⊗ RELEASE) gives the gun back to the AI. The autonomous war keeps running around you — manual control is opt-in, per emplacement, exactly when you want your own hands on a weapon.

v3.6.0 — THE LIVING SKY
A graphics & atmosphere overhaul. The sky now reads as real, layered atmosphere: deep black space at the very top, a drifting nebula and a richer, twinkling, multi-coloured starfield up high, a soft sun glowing in the upper sky, and distinct colour bands flowing down through the mesosphere, stratosphere and troposphere to a warm, glowing horizon that hugs the curved planetary limb. The ground has depth now — layered rock strata and mineral speckle in the subterranean body, exposed ore veins glinting beneath every resource deposit, and a sun-lit topsoil edge. Clouds are fuller and shaded with sunlit crests. And every unit now casts a soft drop-shadow (plus a ground contact shadow), so your forces read clearly against the brightest sky or the blackest space. The world finally looks like the curved, colourful atmospheric battlefield it was always meant to be.

v3.5.0 — TOTAL WAR
A huge combat & systems batch. Point-defence overhaul: CIWS and ALL PD turrets now shoot down incoming enemy MISSILES, not just tungsten rods. Armed populace: every building now defends itself at close range (rifles by default), and cities are fortified and fire back — build a Rotary Cannon Works to upgrade every building & city to CIWS-class rapid flak + missile interception, or a Plasma Bastion Works (Plasma Weapons) for heavy plasma cannons everywhere. Occupied cities stop being shelled and instead get fortified with massive immobile SIEGE GUNS that hammer you (clear them to retake). City losses bite: an occupied city bleeds stability until you retake it, and every destroyed city structure is a PERMANENT stability hit. Underground isn't safe: earthquakes rapidly cave in buried structures, bombs & heavy rounds crack SHALLOW tunnels, and enemies that overrun a buried building breach it (deep buildings resist far more). Combat Drop: a new strategic command deploys YOUR OWN squad anywhere — paratroopers → drop-pods → orbital landings as the ages advance. Scenario Editor (SCENARIOS → ⚙ CUSTOM) lets you dial prep time, difficulty, fleet size, orbital bombardment, ground intensity, skyfall and which superweapons appear, then launch a custom invasion. Hovering a research node or special building now previews its stability impact on the stability readout. Enemy aircraft now weave & bob like real aircraft; antimatter WHISTLER drones lob a finite pool of tiny antimatter rounds, then (or once wounded) dive into a target and detonate; aircraft rearm far faster; enemy drops avoid landing dead-centre in cities; underground hangars cost ~half and burying already-underground buildings (rail hubs, forges, hangars) no longer costs extra; ships no longer get shoved onto land. Plus an achievements framework (🏆 in the footer) ready for medals to come.

v3.4.0 — THE TALL SKY
The skybox is now genuinely TALL — the world is ~1.9× the height of your screen, so the atmosphere stretches from deep black space at the very top, down through the mesosphere, stratosphere and troposphere to the ground far below. The camera now pans VERTICALLY as well as horizontally: it auto-follows the action (sitting on the ground fight by default, rising to frame high-altitude threats when the sky is the battlefield), and you can take manual control any time with W/S, ↑/↓, the mouse wheel, or the top/bottom screen edges. Re-entry now reads as a real plunge — a dropship takes the better part of ten seconds to fall the whole way down, giving your exosphere-reaching weapons (railguns, lasers, orbital guns, satellites) a long window to shoot it apart before it ever lands. Orbital lances and the deep-space stars, space-elevator tether and layer guides all live in their proper place in the much bigger sky.

v3.3.1 — BOMBARDMENT RULES
Orbital bombardment is now reserved for the heavy intents. A Probing Attack (Recruit) and a Decapitation Strike (Officer) no longer rain orbital ordnance at all — a probe is just a ground & air feeler, and a decapitation is a precision capital drop, not a barrage. Only a FULL INVASION (General / "total war") and ORBITAL ANNIHILATION (Apocalypse / glassing) bring the falling bombpods and tungsten-rod barrage. So the lighter difficulties are about holding the line against landings, and the orbital sky-fire is the signature of the worst two.

v3.3.0 — HOSTILE TAKEOVER
The autodrones now read as the enemy: every ENEMY GROUND unit (landed tanks, walkers, pelters, earthbreakers, shield strikers, the Land Leviathan) is repainted a reddish dark-grey so you can tell hostile armour from your own at a glance. A captured city is no longer just a flag — when a region falls, the enemy SEIZES the factories, plants and industry inside it and turns your own war economy against you: those buildings stop producing for you (no output, no garrison) and start spitting out enemy ground forces, marked with a red "◗ SEIZED" flag. Retake the region with ground troops to bring your industry back online. SMARTER TARGETING: turrets and units now de-conflict their fire — before committing a shot they check how much fire is already inbound on a target, so a line of laser/flak turrets spreads across an incoming barrage instead of dumping ten shots into one tungsten rod and letting the rest through (big upgrade for point-defence vs. orbital lances). All weapons also fire with a small randomized timing jitter (up to ±0.3s) so a formation no longer fires in robotic lock-step. WARSHIPS got a cleaner hull — a sleeker, less-blocky silhouette with a raked bow, faceted stealth superstructure and flush VLS.

v3.2.1 — FORTIFY
INFANTRY are stronger (more HP, harder-hitting) and now genuinely earn their keep: a dug-in ground unit AUTO-BUILDS its own emplacements — MG nests, flak/AA nests and field-gun artillery — so a held line grows into a real mini-fort (the emplacements crumble if the unit dies or marches off). STABILITY OFFSET reworked per request: dangerous research now PERMANENTLY lowers stability — it does NOT auto-heal. The only way to recover it is running stability-boosting STRUCTURES (Comms Towers, Radar, and the new PROPAGANDA BUREAU which strongly raises stability). So a late-game research spree quietly kneecaps your stability → influence → research speed unless you invest in keeping the populace in line. New POINT-DEFENCE: the CIWS "Sentry" — a cheap rotary-autocannon turret (no research) that throws a wall of flak to shoot down incoming bombs/missiles and intercept falling TUNGSTEN RODS & orbital lances. Screen your cities with them.

v3.2.0 — DOCTRINE BALANCE
A pass to make the non-laser/plasma paths pull their weight, plus difficulty pacing. PREP TIME is now set by the fleet's intent: a Probing strike gives you only 200s, a Decapitation the usual 135s, a Full Invasion a long 450s, and Annihilation a full 500s to dig in. MECHANICAL & vat-grown units (drones, androids, mechs, satellites, clones) no longer count against your army cap — only manpower-crewed units do, so synthetic armies can swarm. STABILITY OFFSET is back: dangerous research now costs a recoverable unrest offset that decays over ~10s (shown as ⚠ on the stability readout) instead of a permanent loss — riskier doctrines sting, then heal. New NANITE TURRET (Nanotech) fires nanobot rounds that disassemble heavy armour — a strong defensive line off the nanotech path, no Nanite Foundry needed. ENTRENCHMENT: ground units that hold position dig in for a damage-reduction bonus, and standing in a building's footprint (especially an economic block or bunker) GARRISONS it for deep cover — build your own mini-forts. Airbases now keep a mixed air patrol (interceptors + strike jets + helis), and special-resource buildings field a small thematic garrison (cloning vats → clones, gene facility → shock troops, drone line → combat drones, etc.). Retextured the Stealth, Laser Plane, Heavy Bomber and Strike Jet so they no longer share sprites.

// HOW TO COMMAND — AND WHERE

① ECONOMY

IND. CAP builds everything, MANPOWER crews it, INFLUENCE buys research & caps your army — and influence comes only from STABILITY (see ⑦). First thing: open the ECON tab (bottom-left build bar) and place a Power Plant + Housing, then a Comms Tower to lift your stability cap.

② PRODUCE UNITS

Build a Factory / Airbase / Shipyard / Space Center (build bar), then click PRODUCE (top bar) and pick a unit — it rolls out of the matching building. Anything you research appears here too.

③ RESEARCH

Click RESEARCH and spend INFLUENCE on the tech tree. Doctrines unlock whole new armies — drones, androids, psionics, superhumans — and the ★ superweapons. Click several nodes to queue them; they auto-start as influence accrues.

④ ☢ STRATEGIC STRIKES

Build a Missile Silo (DEF tab), then click ☢ STRIKE → choose a weapon → click the battlefield. Your launch sites are marked ▲. Strikes cost STABILITY, not a long reload — call them whenever the line breaks.

⑤ SPECIAL RESOURCES

Build Extractors on the glowing deposits. Owning Steel / Oil / Rare / Uranium permanently cheapens your units and softens the stability cost of strikes — they decide everything late-game.

⑥ ★ SUPERWEAPONS & THE MIND-FORGE

Superweapons (Colossus, Titan, Telekinetic Pylon, Dreadnought…) are built from PRODUCE once researched. The MIND-FORGE plasma lance appears in ☢ STRIKE once you have Antimatter + Telekinesis researched and own Uranium.

⑦ STABILITY → INFLUENCE

Stability is your political capital and the only source of INFLUENCE — the higher your stability, the faster influence (and thus research & army cap) flows. Stability climbs slowly toward your STABILITY CAP, shown as cur / cap in the top bar. You don't gain stability directly from buildings — instead Comms Towers, Propaganda Bureaus & Radar raise the CAP (the ceiling it can reach). Stability falls when cities are hit, you over-use strikes, or run dystopian/psionic doctrine; dangerous research adds a temporary ⚠ unrest that now recovers on its own. If stability hits 0, you lose.

⊕ FIRE CONTROL — HAND-AIM YOUR HEAVY GUNS

Heavy emplacements (Bunker, Laser Tower, Orbital Gun, Mass Driver, Microrocket Battery, Particle Cannon) can be hand-directed. 1) Click the gun to select it → in its info panel press ⊕ ADD TO FIRE CONTROL. A radar ring + a top banner appear. 2) Click enemies inside the radar ring to mark them (a ◎ TARGET reticle appears). 3) The gun then fires ONLY at what you've marked — perfect for saving a railgun shot for a tungsten rod instead of wasting it on chaff. Add more guns to build a battery on one radar. A gun in Fire Control holds its fire until you designate a target — so if nothing happens, you just haven't marked an enemy yet (and there must be one in range). Right-click or Esc releases.

⑧ BUILDINGS & THEIR COSTS

Every structure costs IC + MANPOWER and a small amount of INFLUENCE to authorize. Research Labs no longer make influence — instead each one makes research faster and cheaper. As you advance the ages, your buildings take on a cyberpunk neon look.
